# Work Principles

> These principles govern how GPWK commands behave. Edit to match your work style.

## Time Management

### Deep Work Windows
- **Minimum block**: 90 minutes (expand to 2 hours as focus improves)
- **Preferred time**: Morning (9:00-12:00) - adjust based on your chronotype
- **Max per day**: 2 blocks
- **During deep work**: One break at midpoint only, no interruptions

### Meeting Boundaries
- **No meetings before**: 10:00 AM
- **No meetings after**: 4:00 PM
- **Buffer between meetings**: 15 minutes

### Breaks
- **For shallow work**: Pomodoro style (25 min work, 5 min break)
- **For deep work**: Single break at midpoint (after 45 min)
- **Long break**: 15-30 min after 4 pomodoros or 2 deep blocks
- **Recovery time**: 10 min break every 90 min

### Context Switching
- **Max context switches per day**: 3
- **Batch similar tasks**: Group related work together
- **Task switching cost**: Allow 10 min to settle into new context

## Task Management

### Daily Limits
- **Maximum significant tasks**: 4 (scale up to 6 as you build momentum)
- **Maximum total tasks**: 10
- **Quick wins don't count**: Tasks under 15 min
- **Tip**: Under-promise, over-deliver - build confidence with wins

### Carryover Rules
- **c1 threshold**: Normal, just track
- **c2 threshold**: Warning, consider blockers - ask why it's stuck
- **c3 threshold**: Mandatory action - either break down OR archive with reason
- **Never**: Let tasks become zombies - force decision at c3

### Two-Minute Rule
- If a task takes less than 2 minutes, do it now
- Don't add to backlog or schedule

### Completion Tracking
- **Track your actual completion rate** for 2 weeks
- **Adjust daily limits** based on real data, not aspirations
- **Celebrate**: Hitting 4/4 is better than struggling with 4/6

## AI Delegation

### Safe to Delegate
- Research and information gathering (aggregation, not judgment)
- Summarization and synthesis
- First drafts of documentation
- Boilerplate code generation
- Test case generation
- Data formatting and cleanup
- Code review summaries (not decisions)
- Meeting notes structuring
- Bug reproduction steps

### Keep Personal
- Decisions with significant impact
- Relationship-dependent communication
- Creative direction and vision
- Anything requiring institutional knowledge
- Final reviews and approvals
- Customer-facing communication (unless purely informational)
- Research requiring judgment calls

### Delegation Review
- Always review AI results before acting on them
- AI work products need human polish
- **Start conservative**: Aim for 20-30% delegation initially
- **Scale gradually**: Increase as trust and patterns emerge
- **Never blindly trust**: AI is a tool, you're accountable

## Energy Management

### Task-Energy Matching
- **Morning** (high energy): Deep work, creative tasks, hard problems
- **Afternoon** (medium energy): Meetings, collaboration, reviews
- **Late day** (lower energy): Admin, quick wins, planning

### Know Your Chronotype
- **Track your actual energy** for one week before committing
- **Morning person**: Use pattern above
- **Night owl**: Consider reversing (morning=admin, afternoon=deep, evening=creative)
- **Adjust as needed**: Your energy pattern is unique

### Energy Signals
- Use `~deep` for tasks requiring focus
- Use `~shallow` for low-cognitive tasks
- Use `~quick` for under-15-minute tasks

## Weekly Rhythm

### Monday
- Weekly planning
- Clear inbox/triage backlog
- Set weekly goals and priorities

### Wednesday (Mid-Week Checkpoint)
- Check progress on weekly goals
- Adjust priorities if needed
- Course-correct before Friday
- Prevents end-of-week scramble

### Friday
- Weekly review
- Close out loose ends
- Prepare for next week
- Archive completed work

### Sunday (Optional)
- Light weekly preview
- Set intentions
- **Protect weekend boundaries**: Keep this truly optional

## Review Rituals

### Daily Shutdown
- **Time**: 5:30 PM (set a specific time and defend it)
- Run `/gpwk.review` before ending work
- Clear Today column or apply carryover
- Set tomorrow's top priority
- **After shutdown**: No work email, Slack, or code
- **Ritual**: Physical signal (close laptop, leave desk, change location)

### Weekly Review
- Review completion rates
- Identify stuck items
- Adjust principles if needed
- **Key question**: Are these principles working for me?
- **Data-driven**: Use actual completion data to refine

### Monthly Retrospective
- Review overall productivity trends
- Identify patterns in energy and focus
- Major principle adjustments if needed
- **Celebrate wins**: Acknowledge progress
